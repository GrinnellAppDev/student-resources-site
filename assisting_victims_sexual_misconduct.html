Who needs to report?

All College employees, including student employees or volunteers responsible for the welfare of other students, are requires to report allegations of sexual harassment and sexual misconduct to the College authorities designated herein: Title IX Coordinator

(x4999)

, Campus Safety & Security

(x4600)

, Dean of the College

(x3100)

, Dean of Students

(x3714)

, and/or Director of Human Resources

(x4974)

. The only exceptions are those few employees who operate with statutorily-protected confidentiality (i.e., health services, counseling, clergy, sexual assault advocates). If you have questions about whether to report, please call the Title IX Coordinator.

Why do I need to report?

Reporting is important because it ensures that the complainant has access to all of the avaliable resources, helps to identify individuals displaying patterns of behavior, aids in addressing any trends or systematic problems, and assists in keeping our campus safe.


Respond & Refer
  • Listen and offer them your support. Let them know there are resources avaliable to help them invluding confidentital, campus, and community resources.
  • The person's health and safety should be your primary concern. If the person's safety is an immediate concern, contact Campus Safety & Security at

    x4600

    or

    911

    .
  • Inform them you don't have all the resources that the institution can provide. Contact the Title IX Coordinator who will provide them with options and will keep their information private and only share it with those who "need to know".
  • Remind them that they have the right to choose to whom they will speak, what resources they will use, and what their next steps will be.
  • Report this incident to Title IX in accordance with their policy at the top of this page.
